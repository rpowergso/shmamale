STEP 1

Keep your score low

Every card left in your grid becomes points at the end of the round. Low cards help, high cards hurt, and empty spaces are worth zero.

Your mission Finish rounds with the lowest total and avoid reaching the room's bust score.

Card values: Ace is 1, number cards use their number, Jack is 11, Queen is 12, black King is 13, red King is −1, and the room sets jokers to −2 or 0.

Rooms can end when the first player busts or continue until only one player is left standing.

STEP 2

Learn your opening cards

Each round begins with a face-down grid. Under the default rules, you may privately peek at your bottom two cards before taking your first action.

Custom rooms The host can change the grid and decide which positions let you peek at your own cards or an opponent's cards.

Remember what you see. The cards turn face down again, but their positions still matter.

STEP 5

Take a look at the keybinds

You can play the whole game from the keyboard. Player numbers stay consistent, and 1 always brings the focus back to your own grid.

1–6 then arrows / WASD

Choose a player, then move the gold focus between their cards.

Space

Select the focused card or confirm the highlighted menu choice.

Enter

Open the draw, discard, and call menu. Press it again after a deck draw to play the held card.

K / ?

Open the full keybind guide during a game. Esc closes menus.

STEP 7

Burn matching ranks

When the top discard matches the rank of a card you know, try to burn it—even outside your normal turn. Suits do not matter.

  • Burn your own matching card to remove it and leave an empty zero-point space.
  • Burn an opponent's card, then give them one of yours.
  • Miss the burn and you take a penalty card. The failed card stays visible through the next player's turn.
Reaction raceIf multiple players burn together, the fastest valid attempt wins and later attempts take a penalty.

STEP 8

Call when your grid is ready

Call at the start of your turn before drawing or taking the discard. Calling protects your cards from opponents' switch abilities.

  • The first call gives every other active player one final turn.
  • Calling uses your turn.
  • After calling, you cannot draw, use abilities, or attempt burns for the rest of the round.
Do not call blindlyIf the first caller has the strictly highest hand, their round points are doubled.

STEP 9

Score, survive, repeat

At round end, all remaining card values are added to each player's running score. Empty slots count as zero. If a hand is negative, every hand is shifted equally until the lowest round score is zero.

Last player standing

Players who reach the bust score become spectators. The final survivor wins.

First player busts

The game ends when anyone busts. The lowest running score wins.
