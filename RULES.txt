SHMAMALE RULES
==============

1. Goal
-------

Shmamale is a low-score card game played over multiple rounds.

- Each player has a running score that starts at 0.
- After each round, each player's round points are added to their score.
- The default target score is 50.
- The first player or players to reach or pass the target score lose.
- If multiple players are over the target, the loser is the over-target player
  with the highest score.


2. Cards And Values
-------------------

The deck uses standard playing cards plus jokers.

Card values:

- Joker: -2
- Red King: -1
- Ace: 1
- 2 through 10: face value
- Jack: 11
- Queen: 12
- Black King: 13

Lower totals are better.


3. Player Boards
----------------

- Each player starts each round with four face-down cards in a 2 by 2 board.
- Slot 1 and slot 2 are the top row.
- Slot 3 and slot 4 are the bottom row.
- Empty slots count as 0 points.
- Penalty cards can add extra slots if a player has no empty slot available.


4. Round Setup
--------------

At the start of each round:

1. Shuffle the deck.
2. Deal four face-down cards to each player.
3. Place the remaining cards face down as the draw pile.
4. Start with an empty discard pile.
5. Clear calls, protection, burns, pending actions, and temporary peeks.

Opening peek:

- Before taking their first turn action of the round, a player may privately
  peek at their two bottom cards.
- The opening peek only applies to the bottom row.
- Once that player draws, takes the discard, or calls, their opening peek ends.


5. Turn Choices
---------------

On your turn, if no burn-give action is waiting, choose one action:

- Call
- Take the top discard
- Draw from the deck


6. Taking The Discard
---------------------

When you take the top discard:

1. Put that card into your hand.
2. Choose one of your live board cards.
3. Put the taken discard into that board slot.
4. Move the replaced board card to the discard pile.

Taking the discard does not trigger a special ability.

The card you just placed from the discard cannot be burned against that same
discard moment.


7. Drawing From The Deck
------------------------

When you draw from the deck, you may either swap or play.

Swap:

1. Put the drawn card into one of your live board slots.
2. Move the replaced board card to the discard pile.
3. End your turn.

Play:

1. Put the drawn card directly on the discard pile.
2. If it has a special ability, you may use or skip that ability.
3. End your turn after the ability is resolved or skipped.

Special abilities only trigger when a card drawn from the deck is played to the
discard pile. They do not trigger when a card is swapped into your board.


8. Special Abilities
--------------------

7 or 8:

- Peek at one of your own board cards.

9 or 10:

- Peek at one opponent's board card.

Jack or Queen:

- Choose two live board cards one at a time.
- Each chosen card is moved into the ability tray face down.
- After the second card is chosen, the two cards switch places.
- The cards may belong to you, opponents, or one of each.

Black King:

- Choose up to two live board cards one at a time.
- Each chosen card is moved into the ability tray face up.
- Decide whether to swap them or leave them where they are.
- A selected King that matches the discard may be burned.
- If either selected card is burned, you may finish inspecting the second card,
  but the remaining card can only be put back and the pair cannot be switched.

Red Kings have no special ability.

If a player has called, their cards are protected from opponents' switch
abilities. The caller may still switch their own cards.


9. Burning
----------

Burning is an anytime action that can happen outside the normal turn flow.

You may attempt a burn when:

- The round is still playing.
- There is a top discard card.
- The current top discard has not already produced a successful burn.
- No burn-give action is waiting.
- You are not currently holding a drawn or taken card.

To burn:

1. Choose a live board card belonging to any player.
2. Reveal that card.
3. Compare its rank to the top discard card.

Suits do not matter for burns. Any 7 matches any other 7, and any joker matches
any other joker.

Only one card may be successfully burned on each discard card. The burned card
is placed on top of the discard pile but cannot itself be used to start another
burn. This prevents chained burns.

Successful burn, your own card:

- Remove your card from your board.
- Place the burned card on top of the discard pile.
- The slot becomes empty.

Successful burn, opponent's card:

- Remove the opponent's matching card.
- Place the burned card on top of the discard pile.
- Give one of your own live cards into that opponent's burned slot, face down.
- Your given slot becomes empty.

Failed burn:

- The revealed card stays on the board face up.
- You take a penalty card from the draw pile.
- The penalty goes into your first empty slot, or into a new extra slot if your
  board is full.

You cannot burn a card that was just placed from taking the discard until a new
discard top is created.


10. Calling
-----------

On your turn, before drawing or taking the discard, you may call.

When you call:

- You become protected from opponents' switch abilities.
- You cannot take any more game actions for the rest of the round, including
  drawing, taking the discard, using abilities, or attempting burns.
- If you are the first caller of the round, every other player gets one final
  turn.
- After those final turns finish, the round ends and scores.
- If someone else already called, calling only gives you protection. It does
  not create another set of final turns.

Calling uses your turn. You do not also draw or take the discard.


11. Scoring A Round
-------------------

When the round ends:

1. Add the values of each player's remaining board cards.
2. Empty slots count as 0.
3. If any hand total is negative, add enough points to every hand so that no
   player's round score is negative.
4. If the first caller has the strictly highest raw hand total, that caller's
   round score is doubled.
5. Add each player's round score to their running score.

The next round starts with a player who had the lowest raw hand total. If the
first caller is tied for lowest, prefer another tied low player to start.


12. Game End
------------

After scoring a round:

- If no player has reached the target score, play another round.
- If one or more players have reached or passed the target score, the game ends.
- Among the players at or over the target, the highest score loses.


13. Quick Reference
-------------------

On your turn:

- Call
- Take discard and swap
- Draw, then swap or play

Anytime, if eligible:

- Burn a board card that matches the discard rank

Special cards when played from a deck draw:

- 7 or 8: peek own
- 9 or 10: peek opponent
- Jack or Queen: switch unseen
- Black King: peek two, then choose whether to switch
